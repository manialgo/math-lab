Where sine and cosine come from

Take a circle of radius 1, centred at the origin, and a point that moves around its edge. The angle θ (theta) is measured from the positive x-axis. As the point moves, its horizontal position is called cosine of θ, and its vertical position is called sine of θ.

That's really the whole idea — sine and cosine are just coordinates of a point going around a circle. Tangent is the ratio of the two: tan θ = sin θ / cos θ, and it tells you the slope of the line from the origin to that point.

Core identities

Pythagorean identity

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

Follows directly from the unit circle — the point's coordinates always satisfy the circle's own equation x² + y² = 1.

Angle sum

sin(A + B) = sin A cos B + cos A sin B
cos(A + B) = cos A cos B − sin A sin B

Tangent

tan θ = sin θ / cos θ

Undefined wherever cos θ = 0 — try dragging the point to 90° or 270° on the Unit Circle tab and watch the tangent value.
