Integration

Pick a curve and a left/right boundary. The shaded region is the definite integral — the running total of the curve's height across that interval.

What a definite integral actually measures

Split the interval between a and b into thin vertical strips, each with height equal to the curve at that point. Add up the areas of all those strips — as they get thinner and more numerous, that sum settles on a single number. That number is the definite integral, written:

∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx

It's the signed area between the curve and the x-axis: positive where the curve sits above the axis, negative where it dips below.

Approximated with Simpson's rule

The shaded area is computed numerically over many strips — the same idea taught with rectangles, just refined.
